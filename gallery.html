Inside UPV
A Glimpse into University of the Philippines Visayas

A? S? Diin na?

The College of Arts and Sciences (CAS) is one of the busiest and most familiar spaces in UPV. Many students spend most of their university years here attending general education and major courses across the humanities, social sciences, and natural sciences. Fonacier Hall and the surrounding areas often become places where friendships begin, org meetings happen, and long study sessions turn into shared campus memories.

Panaw ta pagtapos buhi

Walking through UPV often means being surrounded by trees, open skies, and quiet roads that lead to dormitories and student spaces. These paths are part of daily routines, from early morning walks to late afternoon conversations after class. The Miagao campus is known for its natural environment and peaceful atmosphere, giving students moments to slow down, reflect, and breathe between academic demands. Many consider these walks just as memorable as the classrooms themselves.

Pila ka minutes pa SoTech?

Known by many students as the “bukid” area, the School of Technology sits along the higher and hillier side of the campus. Getting here often means taking a bus or a long walk. The journey uphill has become a shared experience among students. SoTech represents hands on learning in UPV, where technical skills, innovation, and applied science are developed.

Pabaybay ta

The coastal side of UP Visayas shows a different side of campus life. Facing the sea, the fisheries area connects students directly with marine science and the environment that defines the university’s identity. The shoreline, research facilities, and open views of the water create a learning space where classrooms extend into nature. Many students visit this area not only for academic work but also to watch sunsets, relax with friends, and experience the calm rhythm of campus by the sea.
